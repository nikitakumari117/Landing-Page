The Science Behind Supernovas

Supernovas are powerful explosions marking the end of a star's life cycle. They occur when stars exhaust their nuclear fuel, leading to a core collapse or thermonuclear explosion. These cosmic events release immense energy, sometimes outshining entire galaxies.

What is a Supernova?

A supernova is a massive explosion that occurs when a star reaches the end of its life. This event releases an immense amount of energy, sometimes outshining entire galaxies for a short period. Supernovas are critical to the universe, as they are responsible for dispersing elements like carbon, oxygen, and iron into space, which are building blocks for planets and life.
